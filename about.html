Conan Guanqi ZHU's projects

Guanqi ZHU

Digital design consultant, Designer, Researcher

Co-founder, E-LAB, Shenzhen, China

Ph.D. Candidate, University of Queensland, School of Architecture, Australia


Guanqi is an Ph.D. Candidate and an active architect who is pursuing the combination between the traditional architectural design methodology and the computational design technologies. His research field includes digital design, interactive installation, material computation, artificial intelligence, virtual reality, and robotic construction.

In his architectural research projects, using processing, python, programming became an essential part to spring up more possibilities for decision. During the design and construction phase, parametric modeling could be used to optimize the shape of façades, reduce the panel categories, cut the cost and boost the efficiency of construction. When it comes to the design strategy, Guanqi mainly focuses on the reused material and considering utilized them in the facades of the buildings or part of a building. Also, Guanqi is a computational designer who has provided digital design consultant services for several design institutes or studios, specializing in solving practical problems incorporated into algorithm and mathematics logicism.

The projects he has participated in had won many international prizes, including the “Autodesk Excellence Awards (AEC), building category, second place award (top 3 in the world)”; A8 reconstruction design competition, 3rd prize. The research project focusing on the improvement of the living condition in Nantou district, Shenzhen also participated in the UABB (BIENNALE OF URBANISM \ ARCHITECTURE) 2017. The project ‘Transformable space’ that focuses on the improvement of urban space influenced by the development of technology, and the interactive installation ‘Mimosa pudica, were exhibited in Vanke Design Commune in September 2018. He had won the National Olympiad in Informatics in Provinces, first prize, 13th in the province.
